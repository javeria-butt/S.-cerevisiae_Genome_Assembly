# Results Interpretation Guide

This guide explains how to analyze and validate the outputs generated by the VGP pipeline to ensure a high-quality, chromosome-level assembly.

---

## 1. GenomeScope2: The "Genome Preview"

The K-mer Profile Plot visualizes k-mer frequency (**y-axis**) against coverage (**x-axis**).

### Visual Features
* **Orange Peak (near 0x):** Sequencing errors (unique k-mers that don't repeat).
* **First Peak (~25x):** Heterozygous k-mers (present in only one chromosome copy).
* **Second Peak (~50x):** Homozygous k-mers (present in both chromosome copies).
* **Black Line:** The mathematical model fit.

### Our Interpretation
* **Diploid Confirmation:** Two distinct peaks at 25x and 50x confirm a diploid state. ✅
* **Reliability:** A **96.5% Model Fit** indicates the results are highly trustworthy. ✅
* **Data Quality:** A very small error peak confirms the high fidelity of the PacBio HiFi reads. ✅

| Metric | Value | Interpretation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Haploid Length** | 11,743,432 bp | Estimated genome size (Target: ~11.7 Mb) |
| **Heterozygosity** | 0.58% | Very low; the genome is mostly homozygous |
| **Read Error Rate** | 0.00094% | Exceptional read quality |

## 2. Gfastats: Assembly Metrics

These statistics describe the "contiguity" (connectedness) of the assembly before scaffolding.

* **Contig N50:** 50% of the assembly is contained in contigs of this size or larger. 
* **Calculation Logic:** Sort contigs longest to shortest; the length at the 50% cumulative sum mark is the N50.

**Our Results:**
* **Contig Count:** 16 (Hap1) / 17 (Hap2) — highly contiguous, near the expected chromosome number for yeast.
* **N50:** ~922 kb — indicates large, well-assembled sequences.

## 3. BUSCO: Biological Completeness

BUSCO checks for the presence of "Universal Single-Copy Orthologs."

| Color | Category | Meaning | Target |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Blue** | Complete (S) | Found once (Perfect) | >90% |
| **Dark Blue** | Duplicated (D) | Found twice (Potential error) | <5% |
| **Yellow** | Fragmented (F) | Partially found | <5% |
| **Red** | Missing (M) | Not found | <5% |

**Our Results:**
* **Hap1:** C:95.9% [S:94.1%, D:1.8%], F:2.7%, M:1.4% (**Excellent**)
* **Hap2:** C:89.0% [S:87.6%, D:1.4%], F:2.5%, M:8.5% (**Good**)
* *Note: Hap2 is naturally less complete as it represents the alternate haplotype.*

## 4. Merqury: K-mer Spectrum & Phasing

### The ASM Spectrum Plot (Most Critical)
This plot shows which k-mers belong to which assembly:
* **Green Peak (~50x):** Shared k-mers (homozygous regions). **Ours: Large/Balanced.**
* **Red/Blue Peaks (~25x):** Haplotype-specific variants. **Ours: Symmetric.**

## 5. Hi-C Contact Maps: Structural Scaffolding

Contact maps show the 3D physical interactions of DNA.

### Visual Reading
* **Axes:** Genome in linear order.
* **Darker Spots:** Higher frequency of physical contact.
* **Triangular Blocks:** Represent individual chromosomes.

### Before vs. After YaHS
1.  **Step 11 (Initial):** Shows a thin diagonal with many small segments. This confirms the **contigs** are correct but haven't been ordered into chromosomes yet.
2.  **Step 14 (Final):** Shows 16–17 clear, dark triangular blocks. This confirms **successful chromosome-level scaffolding.** ✅
